Barkowsky, Johannes. Einführung in die musikalische
Akustik. Copyright 2009. Florian Noetzel GmbH Verlag der
Heinrichshöfen-Bücher, Wilhelmshaven.

Standard-sized paperback. This one looks good.

2. Duffin, Ross W. How Equal Temperament Ruined Harmony (and Why You
Should Care. Copyright 2007 by Ross Duffin. W.W. Norton & Co. New
York.

Trade paperback. Yellowing cover and pages. The cover contains a quote
from the Wall Street Journal: "Delightfully informative and
provocative." I never find "provocative" to be "delightful". This one
set off my BS meter and I haven't even opened it yet.

3. Eidsheim, Nina Sun. Sensing Sound. Singing and Listening as
Vibrational Practice. Copyright 2015 Nina Sun Eidsheim. Duke
University Press, Durham.

Trade paperback. From the back cover: "In Sensing Sound, Nina Sun
Eidsheim offers a vibrational theory of music that radically
reenvisions how we think about sound, music, and listening. Eidsheim
shows how sound, music and listening are dynamic and contextually
dependent, rather than being fixed, knowable, and constant. She uses
twenty-first century operas by Juliana Snapper, Meredith Monk,
Christopher Cerrone, and Alba Triana as case studies to challenge
common assumptions about sound --- such as air being the defult medium
through which it travels --- and to demonstrate the importance of a
performance's location and reception play in its contingency."

Haluska, Ján. The Mathematical Theory of Tone Systems. Pure and
Applied Mathematics. A Dekker Series of Monographs and
Textbooks. Copyright 2004 by Marcel Dekker, Inc. Ister Science Ltd.,
Bratislava.

Hardcover. This one looks good. Advanced. Lots of math. Would appear
to have been typeset in TeX.

Fauvel, John; Flood, Raymond and Wilson, Robin (ed.). Music and
Mathematics. From Pythagoras to Fractals. Copyright 2003 John Fauvel,
Raymond Flood, and Robin Wilson. Oxford University Press, Oxford.

Trade paperback. Small print. Many illustrations. In a popular style,
but also with equations. Looks promising.

3. Glaeser, Georg. Der mathematische Werkzeugkasten. Anwendungen in
Natur und Technik. 3. Auflage. Copyright 2008 Spektrum Akademischer
Verlag, Heidelberg.

Hardcover. One chapter on music. Modern didactic style. Mathematics
seems rigorous. Lots of illustrations. Apparently typeset in TeX.
